Event 3

Summary Report: “Amazon Web Services (AWS): Enterprise Cloud Architectures and Industry Applications”

Event Objectives

  • Provide students with opportunities to gain real-world insights by learning from AWS experts and engineers who actively use cloud technologies in enterprise environments.
  • Update students on current recruitment trends and practical skill requirements in the IT industry, particularly in Cloud Computing, Data Engineering, and Artificial Intelligence.
  • Bridge the gap between university coursework (such as COS2019 – Cloud Computing Architecture) and real-world industry practices.
  • Inspire students to identify their personal challenges and develop a clear career roadmap for long-term professional growth.

Speakers

  • Nguyen Tran Minh Duy – Industry Liaison Officer, Swinburne Vietnam
  • Nguyen Gia Hung – Head of Solutions Architecture in Vietnam & Cambodia, Amazon Web Services (AWS); Founder of FCAJ
  • Banh Cam Vinh – Senior Data Engineer, Renova Cloud
  • Tran Thi Quynh Nhu – Account Manager, Amazon Web Services (AWS)
  • Nguyen Hoang Khang – Solutions Architect, Cloud Kinetics; Swinburne Vietnam Alumnus (K3)

Key Highlights

IT Job Market and Cloud/AI Trends

  • The cloud job market is expanding rapidly, but expectations have also increased significantly. Even internship positions now often require knowledge of technologies such as Kubernetes.
  • AWS is focusing its investment in Vietnam on three strategic areas:
    • Local Talent Development
    • Infrastructure Expansion (Local Zone)
    • Technical Skill Development (Skilling)
  • AI is reshaping the employment landscape by reducing demand for junior engineers while increasing demand for experienced professionals, creating an inverted talent pyramid. AI-powered recruitment systems also evaluate resumes based on continuous self-improvement and learning activities.
  • Rather than choosing only a technical role, candidates should also specialize in a particular industry (for example, becoming a Data Engineer for the retail industry) to create a stronger professional profile.

The Difference Between Academic and Enterprise Data Engineering

  • At university:
    • Clean datasets
    • Clearly defined requirements
    • Flexible schedules
    • Low consequences for mistakes
  • In enterprise environments:
    • Incomplete or noisy data
    • Frequently changing business requirements
    • Tight deadlines
    • Significant business impact when failures occur
  • Companies care about much more than simply moving data into dashboards. Most engineering effort is dedicated to data quality, failure handling, security, and operational reliability.
  • The most important mindset is “One framework for every platform.” Engineers should understand the core architecture of a data platform—including data ingestion, processing, storage, and analytics—because tools evolve, while architectural principles remain stable.

Overcoming Fear and Creating Opportunities

  • Many students fear making mistakes or being judged, largely due to traditional educational environments. The best way to overcome these fears is through continuous practice, such as treating every public speaking opportunity as an internship.
  • Miscommunication often occurs because different stakeholders focus on different priorities. For example, sales teams emphasize business value, while engineers focus primarily on technical implementation.
  • Instead of competing in the crowded “Red Ocean” by only submitting resumes through job portals, students should create a “Blue Ocean” by networking, engaging in small talk, and increasing their professional visibility.

Mindset for Fresh Graduates in the AI Era

  • The biggest mistake is allowing AI to complete assignments without understanding the underlying concepts, effectively outsourcing one’s own understanding.
  • At university, students pay to make mistakes; in the workplace, professionals are paid to avoid mistakes. Therefore, foundational subjects such as Computer Networks, Data Structures, and Databases remain critically important.
  • Employers generally evaluate candidates based on:
    • Attitude
    • Technical Competence
    • Experience
    • Practical Exposure
    • Personal Qualities
  • Rather than focusing solely on the starting salary, fresh graduates should prioritize gaining experience, expanding their professional network, acquiring knowledge, and achieving continuous personal growth.

Key Takeaways

Mindset and Professional Development

  • The Success Formula:
    Result = Capability × Visibility × Consistency
    Being technically skilled is not enough. Professionals must consistently demonstrate their abilities and maintain continuous improvement.
  • Lifelong Learning: Continuously update resumes, develop side projects, and write technical blogs. AI-powered recruitment systems increasingly evaluate these activities when assessing candidates.
  • Attitude Matters More Than Skills: For entry-level positions, employers value initiative, a willingness to learn, and resilience under pressure more than extensive technical experience.

Technical Architecture

  • Strong foundational knowledge remains the most valuable asset. AI serves only as an amplifier; weak fundamentals inevitably lead to incorrect outputs.
  • Understanding the “DNA” of software systems, such as data platforms, is more important than mastering individual tools. Engineers should focus on architectural principles rather than chasing technology trends.
  • Personal projects can be significantly improved by using AI tools such as Amazon Q to review code and identify production-level issues, including security vulnerabilities and inadequate error handling.

Communication and Career Strategy

  • Understand Business Problems: Software engineers should focus not only on writing code but also on understanding business objectives. Effective communication with different departments is essential for gathering accurate requirements.
  • Embrace Imperfection: Accept the fear of making mistakes and continuously ask “Why?” to develop a deeper understanding of technical and business challenges.
  • The Blue Ocean Strategy: Participate actively in technical communities and professional events, build relationships with industry experts, and pursue opportunities through networking and referrals rather than relying solely on traditional job applications.

Applying to Work

  • Improve Personal Projects: Instead of creating generic applications, align projects with a specific industry domain, such as FinTech or Healthcare, while incorporating comprehensive security mechanisms and error-handling strategies.
  • Increase Professional Visibility: Begin writing technical blogs, publishing well-documented GitHub repositories, and consistently sharing learning experiences with the developer community.
  • Communicate More Proactively: Practice small talk and actively ask questions during seminars and university lectures to expand professional networks.
  • Use AI Responsibly: Treat AI as a mentor for explaining complex concepts and reviewing code rather than allowing it to generate solutions without understanding the underlying logic.

Event Experience

Participating in the workshop “Amazon Web Services (AWS): Enterprise Cloud Architectures and Industry Applications” was a major turning point for me. The professional environment and the honest experiences shared by industry experts challenged many of my previous assumptions about the IT industry and provided a much clearer understanding of real-world expectations.

Understanding the Reality of the Job Market

  • Learning that a single junior position may receive over 1,000 applications, while internship candidates are increasingly expected to understand Kubernetes, made me realize that I must accelerate my learning journey immediately. The statement “Good grades do not necessarily guarantee employment” was particularly eye-opening.

Learning from Industry Experts

  • Listening to Banh Cam Vinh explain the differences between clean academic datasets and messy enterprise data helped me understand why mastering foundational subjects is so important.
  • I was especially impressed by Nguyen Hoang Khang’s story about candidates who completed recruitment coding assessments rapidly using AI but were unable to explain their own solutions afterward. This completely changed my perspective on how I should use AI in the future.

Overcoming Psychological Barriers

  • Tran Thi Quynh Nhu’s presentation about fear and Imposter Syndrome resonated strongly with me. Hearing professionals from major technology companies openly discuss the same fears gave me confidence that these challenges are normal and can be overcome.

Key Takeaways

  • Technology is only a tool; mindset is the true foundation. Rather than constantly chasing the latest technologies or frameworks, students should focus on mastering core subjects such as Computer Networks, Databases, and Data Structures.
  • Never outsource your understanding to AI. AI may complete assignments efficiently, but it cannot replace critical thinking during technical interviews or when solving real production problems.
  • Career opportunities do not appear automatically. Students must continuously invest in learning, build high-quality projects, expand their professional networks, and increase their visibility so that opportunities can naturally find them.
