Event 1

Summary Report: “FCAI Workshop”

Event Objectives

  • Share effective and cost-efficient methods and tools for learning and practicing AWS services, especially for beginners.
  • Inspire students by sharing practical experiences from Hackathon competitions, encouraging them to step outside their comfort zones.
  • Present a real-world case study on developing an AI-powered SaaS application and emphasize the importance of the DevOps mindset in teamwork.
  • Address common psychological barriers faced by students and young professionals, including overcoming Imposter Syndrome, building self-confidence, and breaking the habit of procrastination.

Speakers

  • Huynh Thai Linh – Student
  • The Bowler Team (Huynh An Khuong, Mai Quoc Anh, Nguyen Tran Minh Quan) – FPT University Students
  • Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu – Final-year Student, HUTECH University
  • Tran Nghia – Swinburne Alumnus / AI & Machine Learning Developer (Founder of the Dai Viet Astrology Project)
  • Tran Minh Quan – FPT University Student / Freelance Web Application Developer
  • Pham Phat Uy – Vietnam German University (VGU) Student

Key Highlights

Cost-Effective AWS Learning Strategy

  • Combine AWS Cloud Quest (an interactive 3D learning game) to understand cloud architecture concepts with Floci or LocalStack (AWS service emulators) to test applications locally before deploying them to the real AWS cloud, significantly reducing costs.

Understanding the Value of Hackathons

  • Hackathons are not just coding competitions; they are opportunities to develop resilience under pressure (such as coding continuously for 36 hours during the VNG Lotus Hackathon), expand professional networks, and strengthen a resume.
  • The key lesson is: Do not worry about lacking experience. Participate, learn by doing, and always prepare a product demonstration.

Case Study: “Đại Việt Astrology” AI-Powered SaaS Project

  • Transform traditional astrology knowledge into a modern web application.
  • Address AI hallucination problems by combining deterministic rule-based logic (knowledge extracted from books) with a Vector Database (RAG) before sending requests to the Claude 3.5 Sonnet language model.

The Hidden Iceberg of DevOps

  • Coding errors and deployment failures are only the visible part of a project’s problems. The hidden causes of failure include unclear requirements, poor communication, manual processes, and a lack of ownership.
  • Four core DevOps principles:
    • Collaboration
    • Automation
    • Fast Feedback
    • Continuous Improvement

Applied Psychology: Confidence and Procrastination

  • Confidence: Understanding Imposter Syndrome and the Dunning-Kruger Effect, especially the “Valley of Despair” encountered while learning new skills.
  • Procrastination: Procrastination is not caused by laziness but by psychological barriers such as fear of failure and fear of judgment. The brain naturally seeks short-term comfort to avoid stressful situations.

Key Takeaways

Mindset & Psychology

  • Action creates confidence: Confidence is the result of taking action, not a prerequisite for getting started. Waiting for perfection only delays progress.
  • Understanding procrastination: Learning how the conflict between the prefrontal cortex (long-term planning) and the limbic system (short-term emotions) influences behavior helped me realize that procrastination is a psychological challenge rather than a sign of laziness.

Technology & Architecture

  • Local AWS Environment: Learned how to use Docker, Boto3, Terraform, and Floci to simulate and test more than 50 AWS services locally before deploying them to the cloud.
  • Reliable AI Architecture: Understood the complete AI data processing pipeline: Input → Post Parser → Vector Search (RAG) → LLM → Validation, which significantly improves response accuracy compared to relying solely on prompts.

Teamwork & Personal Development

  • Technical tools such as CI/CD and Docker are important, but people, communication, and the DevOps mindset ultimately determine project success.
  • Learned practical productivity techniques, including:
    • The 5-Second Rule for making immediate decisions.
    • The 5-Minute Rule for overcoming resistance and starting difficult tasks.

Applying to Work

  • Optimize AWS Learning Costs: Install Floci or LocalStack immediately. From now on, all AWS labs will be developed and tested locally before deployment to a real AWS account, ensuring scripts work correctly while minimizing cloud expenses.
  • Expand Networking and Practical Experience: Plan to join a team and participate in at least one Hackathon this year to gain hands-on experience and strengthen my portfolio.
  • Improve Team Collaboration: Apply the DevOps mindset by clarifying requirements and maintaining continuous communication among developers, business analysts, and QA engineers to reduce misunderstandings and rework.
  • Self-Management: Begin using the 5-Minute Rule whenever facing challenging tasks or feeling tempted to procrastinate by browsing social media.

Event Experience

Participating in the “FCAI Workshop” was both inspiring and relatable because the speakers were current students and alumni who openly shared the same challenges that I am experiencing today. Several moments left a strong impression on me.

Learning from Real Failures

  • I was particularly impressed by the story of accidentally leaving AWS resources running, resulting in an unexpected USD 70 cloud bill. Hearing this real experience helped me understand common mistakes beginners should avoid when learning cloud computing.
  • Listening to The Bowler team’s experience of coding continuously for 36 hours during a Hackathon inspired me with their passion, determination, and willingness to challenge themselves.

Exposure to Real-World Technology

  • I was fascinated by the Đại Việt Astrology project. Seeing a traditional cultural topic implemented using modern technologies such as Next.js, Supabase, Large Language Models (LLMs), Vector Databases, and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) demonstrated how creatively technology can be applied across different domains.

A Wake-Up Call About Psychology

  • The presentation on the “Iceberg of Procrastination” felt as though it was describing my own experiences. Instead of offering generic motivational advice, the speaker explained procrastination from a neuroscience perspective, helping me understand why I tend to delay important tasks and how I can overcome this behavior.

Networking and Communication

  • I realized the importance of being proactive and visible. Asking questions and communicating with others is not only a way to solve problems but also the first step toward building professional networks and creating future career opportunities.

Key Takeaways

  • At university, you pay to make mistakes; at work, you are paid to avoid mistakes. Therefore, students should take full advantage of their academic years and personal projects to experiment, learn, and fail without fear.
  • AI is an amplifier, not a replacement for understanding. If your own knowledge is weak, AI-generated results will also be unreliable. AI should support learning rather than replace critical thinking.
  • Do not let fear—whether fear of failure, criticism, or making mistakes—prevent you from moving forward. Start building, keep improving, and learn through action.
